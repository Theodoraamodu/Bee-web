Queen Bee: The queen bee is the largest bee in the colony and plays a crucial role in reproduction. She lays eggs that hatch into worker bees, drones (males), and new queen bees. The queen bee is fed royal jelly throughout her life, which helps her develop reproductive organs and a longer lifespan compared to other bees in the colony.

❮ ❯

Drone bees are male bees whose primary role is to mate with the queen bee during the mating season. They do not have stingers and do not participate in tasks such as foraging or hive maintenance. Once mating is complete, drone bees die, and their role in the colony is primarily focused on reproductive functions.

❮ ❯

Builder Bee: Builder bees, also known as worker bees, are the most numerous bees in a colony. They perform various tasks such as building and maintaining the hive, foraging for food (nectar and pollen), caring for the young bees (larvae), and defending the hive. Worker bees are essential for the survival and functioning of the bee colony.

❮ ❯

Worker Bee: Worker bees are the female bees in the colony that do not lay eggs. They perform a wide range of tasks such as cleaning and maintaining the hive, feeding the larvae, collecting nectar and pollen, producing honey, and guarding the hive against intruders. Worker bees are vital for the overall productivity and efficiency of the colony.
